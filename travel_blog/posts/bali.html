A Weekend in Bali

By Sarah Johnson Published on March 22, 2024

Bali, the Island of Gods, is a paradise of lush greenery, serene beaches, and rich culture. In just 48 hours, you can soak in its beauty and spiritual essence.

Day 1: Temples and Beaches

We started at Uluwatu Temple, perched on a dramatic cliffside, offering breathtaking ocean views. Arriving early allowed us to beat the crowds and enjoy the tranquility.

Afternoon was spent at Padang Padang Beach, a secluded spot perfect for sunbathing and swimming. The evening ended at Jimbaran Bay with a seafood feast by the shore.

Day 2: Ubud's Cultural Heart

We headed to Ubud, the cultural capital of Bali. The Sacred Monkey Forest, lush rice terraces of Tegallalang, and the vibrant Ubud Market made for an unforgettable day.

Our trip concluded with a traditional Balinese massage at a serene spa, leaving us refreshed.

Where to Stay

We stayed in a private villa in Seminyak, known for its lively cafes and boutiques.

Dining Highlights

  • Breakfast: Nalu Bowls - Best smoothie bowls
  • Lunch: Warung Bambu - Authentic Balinese cuisine
  • Dinner: Locavore - Farm-to-table gourmet experience

Travel Tips

  • Rent a scooter for easy transportation
  • Respect temple dress codes
  • Try the famous Luwak coffee
